🥐Croissant

One interesting fact about croissants is that they are not originally from France, despite being strongly associated with French cuisine. Croissants actually originated in Austria during the 17th century. They were inspired by the Austrian pastry called "kipferl," which has a similar crescent shape. The croissant as we know it today became popular in France in the 19th century, thanks to Viennese bakers who brought the recipe and techniques to France.
